Any point \textbf{x} in the LP's feasible region may be written in the form $$\textbf{x = d} + \sum \frac{i=k}{i=1} \sigma_i \textbf{b}_i$$ where \textbf{d} is \textbf{0} or a direction of unboundedness and $\sum \frac{i=k}{i=1}$ $\sigma_i = 1$ and $\sigma_i \geq0.$}} If the LP's feasible region is bounded, then \textbf{d = 0}, and we may write \textbf{x} = $\sum \frac{i=k}{i=1} \sigma_i \textbf{b}_i$, where the $\sigma_i$ are nonnegative weights adding to 1. In this case, we see that any feasible \textbf{x} may be written as a \textbf{convex combination} of the LP's bfs. We now give two illustrations of Theorem 2. Consider the Leather Limited example. The feasible region is bounded. To illustrate Theorem 2, we can write the point $G$ = (20, 10) ($G$ is not a bfs!) in Figure 3 as a convex combination of the LP's bfs.
